How to Afford Tacrolimus (generic)

Tacrolimus (generic) (Tacrolimus) is an immunosuppressant used by transplant patients (Kidney, Liver, Heart, Lung, Pancreas). Here are the ways to lower what you pay, from copay cards to programs that provide it for free.

Estimated discount cash price: $20 to $50/month (varies by pharmacy; your price may be lower with insurance or assistance).

Ways to save on Tacrolimus (generic)

Patient Assistance Program (free medication)

If you are uninsured, underinsured, or on Medicare/Medicaid and meet income limits, you may get Tacrolimus (generic) at no cost. Ask your transplant team about the manufacturer or foundation options for this medication.

Foundation grants

Charitable foundations can help cover copays and other costs, based on income and diagnosis.

Generic version

A generic Tacrolimus is available and usually costs far less. For anti-rejection drugs, stay on the same manufacturer and talk to your transplant team before switching.

Common questions about Tacrolimus (generic)

How can I get Tacrolimus (generic) for free?

If you are uninsured, underinsured, or on Medicare/Medicaid and meet income limits, a Patient Assistance Program (PAP) may provide Tacrolimus (generic) at no cost. Foundation grants can also cover your share of the cost. Take the free 2-minute quiz to see which programs you qualify for.

Is there a generic for Tacrolimus (generic)?

Yes, a generic version of Tacrolimus is available and usually costs much less. For transplant anti-rejection drugs, stay on the same manufacturer and talk to your transplant team before switching, because small changes can affect your drug levels.

How much does Tacrolimus (generic) cost?

Cash prices vary by pharmacy, but an estimated discount price is around $20 to $50/month. Your actual cost depends on your insurance, and copay cards or assistance programs can reduce it further.
