# Transplant Medication Navigator — Funder & Partner Target List

Purpose: who funds patient education on transplant medication access, what kind
of money each one gives, who to approach, and what to ask for. Lead with the
warm relationships; use a society co-sign (AST) to unlock the pharma money.

**Golden rule that keeps you fundable:** education stays independent and
program rankings stay neutral. Disclose every funder. No funder may buy
placement over a clinically appropriate option. This independence is exactly
what IME reviewers and societies require.

---

## Tier 0 — Warm leads (start here)

### American Society of Transplantation (AST)
- **Money type:** society endorsement / educational partnership / co-branded
  content. Small direct dollars, but huge credibility that unlocks pharma IME.
- **Who:** AST Education Committee / Health Equity & Access initiatives; their
  patient-education staff.
- **Ask:** an educational collaboration + endorsement of the platform's patient
  medication-access education. Use their name (with permission) on grant
  applications to pharma.
- **Why it matters:** an AST co-sign turns a cold IME application into a
  credible one. Lock this first and reference it everywhere.

### UT Southwestern Medical Center (Clements University Hospital)
- **Role:** flagship pilot site (B2B) + academic credibility for grants.
- **Money type:** B2B program license (Channel 1) AND a named pilot site on
  education grants.
- **Who:** transplant program administration; financial navigation / social
  work; transplant pharmacy.
- **Ask:** a pilot of the Epic-integrated tool with their financial navigators;
  permission to measure time saved + dollars secured for a case study.

### UC San Diego — Lung Transplant Program
- **Role:** pilot site with a **lung-specific** angle (you just built out the
  full PAH/CF/antifibrotic catalog — lead with that).
- **Money type:** B2B license + lung-focused education pilot.
- **Who:** lung transplant program director / coordinator; pharmacy.
- **Ask:** pilot the lung medication catalog + import flow; co-develop a
  lung-transplant medication-access education module (a natural IME topic for
  United Therapeutics / J&J / Vertex).

### CareDx (Nasdaq: CDNA)
- **Role:** strategic transplant-industry partner — NOT an IME grant. CareDx
  lives in transplant patient surveillance/engagement (AlloSure, AlloMap,
  AlloCare patient app, transplant pharmacy services). Medication access +
  adherence is squarely in their interest.
- **Money type:** commercial sponsorship / co-marketing / integration
  partnership; potentially strategic investment.
- **Who:** Patient Solutions / Digital Health / Partnerships / Corporate
  Development.
- **Ask:** sponsorship of the platform + exploration of integration with their
  patient app/care pathway. Frame around shared goal: keep transplant patients
  on therapy.
- **Caution:** keep neutrality intact; a CareDx relationship cannot bias which
  medications/programs surface. Disclose the relationship.

## Tier 1 — Pharma Independent Medical Education (IME) grants

Cleanest pharma money: firewalled, non-promotional, application-based via each
company's grant portal. Target the manufacturers of drugs already in the app.
Typical range ~$10k–$250k per program; requires a documented educational gap.

| Company | Transplant drug(s) in app | Approach via |
|---|---|---|
| **Astellas** | Prograf / tacrolimus (market leader) | Independent Medical Education grant portal; Medical Affairs |
| **Novartis** | Myfortic, cyclosporine, Zortress | IME / Medical Education grants |
| **Veloxis** | Envarsus XR | Medical Affairs (smaller, transplant-focused) |
| **Sanofi** | Thymoglobulin | IME grants |
| **Takeda** | Livtencity (maribavir) | IME / patient education grants |
| **Merck** | Prevymis (letermovir), Winrevair | Medical Education grants |
| **Genentech/Roche** | CellCept legacy, Valcyte, Esbriet | Independent Medical Education |
| **United Therapeutics** | Tyvaso, Remodulin, Orenitram, Adcirca (PAH) | Medical/patient education — lung angle |
| **J&J / Janssen (Actelion)** | Opsumit, Uptravi, Ventavis, Opsynvi (PAH) | IME grants — lung angle |
| **Vertex** | Trikafta, Kalydeco (CF) | Medical/patient education — lung/CF angle |

> Separate, branded pool: each company's **brand / Patient Support Program
> team** has larger budgets for copay-card/PAP *awareness*. Branded + disclosed,
> never ranked above better options. Approach only after the IME relationship.

## Tier 3 — Payers & specialty pharmacy

- **Health plans (Medicare Advantage, Medicaid managed care):** fund member
  education because adherence drives **Star ratings** and lowers cost. Strong,
  underused. Approach: pharmacy / quality / Stars teams.
- **Specialty pharmacies (Accredo, CVS Specialty):** adherence/education
  budgets — **Anti-Kickback caution** if anything touches referrals.

## Tier 4 — Government / research

- **HRSA** (organ transplantation), **PCORI** (patient-centered outcomes),
  **NIH / NIDDK** — research-leaning, but patient-education components exist and
  add prestige.

## Sequencing

1. **Lock AST** endorsement/partnership.
2. **Sign UT Southwestern + UC San Diego** as pilot sites (also B2B revenue).
3. **Bring CareDx** in as a strategic sponsor/integration partner.
4. With AST + two academic sites named, **apply to Astellas / Novartis IME**
   (kidney/general) and **United Therapeutics / J&J / Vertex** (lung).
5. Layer a **society/foundation** co-sign for additional credibility.
