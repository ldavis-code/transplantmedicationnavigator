Your denial letter tells you why they said no. Here are the most common reasons:

Prior Auth Needed

Your doctor needs to ask permission first before the insurance will cover it.

Not on the List

Your drug isn't on their approved list (called a formulary).

Try Something Else First

They want you to try a cheaper drug first (step therapy).

Quantity Limits

They say you're getting too much of this drug.

Use the Generic

They want you to use a cheaper generic version.

Remember: A denial is NOT the final answer. It's just the first "no", and you can fight it!

Write a Letter to Your Doctor

Ask your doctor to help with your appeal

Why write to your doctor? Your doctor has the medical knowledge to explain why you need your specific medication. A letter of medical necessity from your doctor is often the key to winning an appeal.

Note: Your doctor already has access to a detailed medical necessity letter template. This letter is for you to request their help and provide them with the information they need.

Before you continue: The details you type here (your name, medication, transplant type and date, and denial details) are used only on this device to build your letter. They are not sent to or stored on our servers. Do not include information that is not needed for the letter. Read our full Privacy Policy.

How to Appeal: A Step-by-Step Guide

Medicare Appeals

Important: Act on time.

  1. Coverage Decision: Ask your plan to pay for your choice of pharmacy.
  2. Level 1 (Second Look): File within 65 days if they say no.
  3. Level 2 (Outside Review): An outside group looks at your case if denied again.
Official Medicare Guide

Medicaid Appeals

Each state runs its own appeals. Call your state's Medicaid office.

Private Insurance

You can ask your company to look again. If they still say no, you can ask for an outside review.

Remember: You Are Not Alone

Thousands of transplant patients successfully appeal their denials every year. Your health matters, and you have every right to fight for the medications you need. Stay strong and don't give up!
