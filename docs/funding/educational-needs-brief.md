## The educational gap (unmet need)

Transplant recipients must take immunosuppressants and supportive medications
**for the life of the graft**, yet face two compounding barriers:

1. **Cost / financial toxicity.** Specialty immunosuppressants and adjuncts can
   cost thousands per month. Patients frequently don't know that copay cards,
   patient-assistance programs (PAPs), foundation grants, and cash-price options
   exist — or which ones apply to *their* specific drug.
2. **Complexity & confusion.** Generic vs. brand (e.g., tacrolimus vs. Prograf,
   mycophenolate sodium vs. Myfortic) changes which assistance applies; brand
   copay cards don't cover generics, and patients often can't tell the
   difference. Misunderstanding drives **cost-related non-adherence**.

**Why it matters clinically:** medication non-adherence is a leading,
*modifiable* cause of late acute rejection and graft loss. Education that closes
the access gap directly supports adherence, graft survival, and reduced
readmissions.

## Target audience

- **Primary:** pre- and post-transplant patients (kidney, liver, heart, lung,
  pancreas) and their caregivers.
- **Secondary:** transplant financial navigators, social workers, and pharmacists
  who counsel patients on access.

## Educational objectives

After engaging with the program, a patient/caregiver will be able to:
1. Identify their specific medications (incl. brand vs. generic) and what that
   means for assistance eligibility.
2. Locate the right copay card, PAP, foundation, or cash-price option for each
   medication.
3. Recognize and resolve cost-related barriers *before* they cause a missed dose.

## Delivery & differentiator

A free, patient-facing web platform that:
- Lets patients **import their own medication list read-only from Epic MyChart**
  (standalone patient launch + provider-side Epic EHR launch), so guidance is
  personalized, not generic.
- Maps each medication to its assistance pathways, correctly distinguishing
  **brand vs. generic** so patients are steered to the right option.
- Flags medications **not yet covered** so patients know to seek them out.
- Integrates into the transplant-center workflow for financial navigators.

## How impact is measured (outcomes)

The platform already captures, in aggregate and de-identified:
- **Assistance connections made** — copay-card, PAP, and foundation click-throughs.
- **Estimated dollars saved** — original vs. paid price per fill, average savings
  per fill, savings by program type.
- **Engagement** — quiz starts/completions, medication searches, distinct users.
- **Unmet need** — most-requested medications not yet in the database.

Reported back to funders as a de-identified impact summary each quarter.
